# V2.1 Basic Booking Self-Service

## Purpose

This milestone is about **operational correctness**.

It is the point at which the Tracker becomes trustworthy enough for real booking use by getting the basics right:

- the right people booked
- into the right sessions
- in the right numbers
- with the right parent/child relationships
- and with cancellations and attendance changes reflected truthfully over time

This milestone is not mainly about adding booking convenience. It is about making the booking picture accurate enough to support real operational decisions.

## Charity value

This milestone should create charity value by:

- helping DTV control dig sizes more effectively
- reducing avoidable admin work around bookings and changes
- making cancellations easier and more normal, reducing blocked places and no-shows
- handling the parent/child case more safely and realistically
- improving confidence that the attendance picture shown by the Tracker reflects reality closely enough to act on

## Charity burden effect

This milestone should reduce burden more than it adds.

It should:

- reduce manual back-and-forth around routine booking changes
- reduce ambiguity about who is actually booked on
- improve the charity's ability to manage numbers without relying solely on manual checks
- preserve fallback options such as Eventbrite and paper registration while the Tracker earns a larger operational role

It should avoid:

- making the Tracker the only viable booking path too early
- forcing group or corporate booking workflows into self-service before they are ready
- overcomplicating admin-side limits management in the first cut
- locking in a weak or misleading attendance model based on deleting meaningful booking history

## What completion looks like

When this milestone is complete:

- a self-service volunteer can book onto a suitable session through the Tracker
- a self-service volunteer can cancel their booking through the Tracker
- parent/child booking works in a way that reflects real responsible-adult relationships
- session spaces and limits are clear enough to support real attendance control
- bookings, cancellations, attendance and no-shows are represented truthfully enough for the Tracker to be trusted operationally
- the charity has a better handle on numbers without needing to remove Eventbrite or paper fallbacks immediately

## What this milestone does not try to do

This milestone does not aim to:

- fully replace Eventbrite
- make the Tracker the sole operational path for volunteer attendance
- cover group or corporate bookings in self-service
- deliver broader volunteer coordination across groups, projects or ad hoc sessions
- deliver advanced messaging, targeting, or funnel-management functionality

## Key operational focus

The core focus of this milestone is operational correctness across four areas:

### 1. Booking correctness
- the correct person is booked
- parent/child relationships are represented properly
- self-service does not distort admin-managed cases

### 2. Capacity correctness
- spaces and limits mean something real
- children and adults are counted properly
- the session picture is accurate enough to manage dig size safely

### 3. Change correctness
- cancellation does not simply make important history disappear
- cancellations, attendance and no-shows are not treated as the same thing
- Eventbrite interaction does not create a false impression of certainty

### 4. Visibility correctness
- what the charity sees in the Tracker matches operational reality closely enough to support decision-making

## Notes for Trustees

This milestone should be seen as the point where the Tracker becomes operationally trustworthy for basic booking use.

It is not yet the milestone where the system starts coordinating volunteers more strategically across the whole charity. Instead, it focuses on getting the booking model correct enough to reduce confusion, improve attendance accuracy, and help DTV manage session sizes more reliably.

Through the charity lens, the value of this milestone is not simply that it adds self-service booking. Its value is that it improves the accuracy and usefulness of the charity's operational picture without overcommitting the organisation to a more complex or fragile system too early.
