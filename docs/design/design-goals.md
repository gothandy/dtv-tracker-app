# DTV App Design Goals

## Purpose

The DTV app is a self-service volunteer platform.

Its job is to reduce the admin burden on the volunteers doing behind-the-scenes coordination, while making it easier for volunteers to do more for themselves.

The app should help DTV:

- reduce manual admin
- reduce reliance on Eventbrite, especially for returning users
- make booking and repeat attendance more direct
- improve communication with volunteers
- support more volunteer self-service over time
- create a stronger ongoing relationship with volunteers beyond one-off event sign-up

This is not just a logged-in area. It is part public front door, part practical volunteer tool.

## Product context

Use this framing:

- Website = broad public invitation
- App = self-service volunteer space with a public front door

The website is there to attract, explain and reassure.

The app is there to help people actually do things with less admin friction.

That means the app needs to work in two modes:

1. Public-facing pages that welcome, explain and signpost the next step
2. Logged-in pages that feel practical, personal and operational

The app should feel like the same organisation as the website, but not the same thing.

## Design goals

### 1. Make self-service easy

The app should help volunteers do routine things without needing manual help.

Examples include:

- registering
- logging in
- booking onto sessions
- checking bookings
- cancelling or changing plans
- seeing what happens next
- uploading photos
- checking history, hours or milestones

### 2. Reduce hidden admin work

Behind-the-scenes volunteers should not need to chase routine actions that the app can handle clearly and simply.

The design should reduce avoidable back-and-forth and make the volunteer journey obvious.

### 3. Support repeat volunteers better than Eventbrite can

The app should not just copy an event booking flow. It should support an ongoing relationship.

It should become easier for returning volunteers to:

- book again
- understand their status
- see relevant next steps
- feel recognised
- stay connected to what DTV is doing

### 4. Keep a public front door

The app has public-facing pages and direct-entry pages.

People may land here from:

- social media
- email links
- follow-up messages
- search engines
- shared links

Those pages should still feel welcoming and should always make the route to **log in**, **register** or **book your first dig** clear.

### 5. Create a foundation for growth

The app should not be designed only around today’s simple event-booking model.

As DTV grows, the work will become more varied and more complex. Regular digs may need to split into multiple teams, and DTV will need more volunteers who can help on a more flexible or ad hoc basis with planning, preparation, coordination and other behind-the-scenes tasks.

The app should reduce admin burden now, while creating a foundation for coordinating more volunteers, more roles, more teams and more types of contribution over time.

## What the app should feel like

The app should feel:

- grounded
- outdoorsy
- sturdy
- direct
- friendly
- volunteer-led
- real, not polished
- useful, not corporate

It should not feel like:

- a startup dashboard
- a generic events platform
- a polished charity portal
- a charity CRM
- a lifestyle app

## Core principles

### Same organisation, different job

The app should feel like part of the same DTV world as the website, but built for practical use.

### Same brand, less shouty, more useful

The website can do more of the inviting and selling.

The app should be calmer and more controlled, while still feeling bold and recognisable.

### One clear next step

Each page should make the main next action obvious.

Do not make users hunt for the thing they are supposed to do.

### Public pages should always signpost onward

Every public-facing page should clearly offer a route to:

- log in
- register
- or take the most sensible next step

### Build for today, leave room for tomorrow

The interface should stay simple now, but the product structure should leave room for more teams, more roles, more volunteer pathways and more self-service over time.

## Brand relationship to the website

The app should carry over the strongest parts of the website’s visual language:

- DTV green as the main brand colour
- dark anchor panels
- strong contrast
- real volunteer photography
- bold, practical layouts
- rugged brand type used sparingly

The app should not simply mirror the website.

The website is the public invitation.

The app is the sign-on desk, briefing board and volunteer workspace.
